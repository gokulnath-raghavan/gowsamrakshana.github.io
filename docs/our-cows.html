Our Cows

Meet the divine Gow matas under our care and protection

The Sacred Beings We Protect

At Sri Vijaya Lakshmi Gow Samrakshana Trust, we currently shelter and care for more than 100 cows. Most of these cows were abandoned by their owners after they stopped giving milk or became old. Many were rescued from roads where they were vulnerable to accidents or from falling into the hands of butchers.

According to our ancient texts, the cow is sacred and considered the mother of the universe. Our goshala is dedicated to protecting these divine beings and providing them with dignity and care for their entire lifetime.

Our Facility

Our 8-acre facility provides ample space for our cows to roam freely and graze on fresh grass. We have constructed proper shelters to protect them from harsh weather conditions.

  • Green pastures for grazing
  • Covered shelters for protection
  • Clean water sources
  • Medical care facilities
  • Feeding stations

Gallery of Our Cow Family

Daily Care and Routine

Morning Care

Early morning feeding with nutritious hay and fodder, followed by health check-ups and cleaning of the shelters.

Daytime Activities

Cows are allowed to graze in the open pastures under supervision, with access to shade and water.

Evening Routine

Evening feeding, medical treatments if needed, and securing the cows in shelters for the night for protection.

Medical Care

We have an experienced veterinary doctor who regularly checks our cows' health and provides necessary treatments. Our medical care includes:

  • Regular health check-ups
  • Vaccination and deworming
  • Treatment for injuries and illnesses
  • Proper nutrition management
  • Special care for elderly cows
