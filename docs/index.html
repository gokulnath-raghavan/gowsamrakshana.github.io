Our Mission

Sri Vijaya Lakshmi Gow Samrakshana Trust is dedicated to rescuing, rehabilitating, and protecting abandoned desi cows. Located on SH 106, 17km from Thiruthani towards Podaturpet, our goshala provides a safe haven for cows that have been abandoned by their owners, often after they stop giving milk.

Currently, we are caring for around 100 Gow matas, treating them with the respect and dignity they deserve in our Indian tradition. Our 8-acre facility provides green pastures for grazing and proper shelter for all our rescued cows.

This Gowshala is maintained and run by Saints and Veda Pandits who are deeply committed to the protection of these sacred animals.

Rescue & Rehabilitation

We rescue abandoned cows from roads and highways, saving them from potential accidents and butchers. Each rescued cow receives immediate medical attention and care.

Care & Protection

Our cows receive nutritious food, clean water, regular health check-ups, and a loving environment. We have an experienced veterinary doctor to ensure their well-being.

Education & Awareness

We educate visitors about the cultural and spiritual significance of cows and the importance of their protection in our tradition and ecosystem.

Our Current Challenge

After being hit suddenly and harshly by the COVID crisis, we are struggling to sustain our operations. We currently face a deficit of 1 lakh 20 thousand rupees to maintain proper care for our rescued cows.

Daily expenses include feed, medical care, shelter maintenance, and staff salaries. Your support, no matter how small, can help us continue our mission of protecting these sacred animals.
